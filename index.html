Gun deaths in Florida drops after the gun control?

Presented by Jessie Pang

Orginal graph by Reuters:


Explanation

To many, this graph shows huge decrease in the number of gun deaths after Stand Your Ground was enacted. But it's not true. The y-axis reveals that the gun deaths are counted upside down, i.e. from top (0) to bottom (800). The highest peaks are the fewest gun deaths and the lowest ones are the most. A rise in this graph actually means a decrease in gun deaths.

Flipped both horizontally and vertically — is more intuitive to most: a rising line reflects a rise in the number of gun deaths and a dropping a drop.

Conclusion

The proper conclusion is that gun deaths skyrocketed after Stand Your Ground was enacted.
This example is a great reminder that we bring our own assumptions to our reading of any illustration of data. The original graph may have broken convention, making the intuitive read of the image incorrect, but the data is, presumably, sound. It’s our responsibility, then, to always do our due diligence in absorbing information. The alternative is to be duped.
